Soft Pretzels

This is one of the easiest ways to prepare homemade soft pretzels and the results are extra delicious. The soft pretzel dough only needs to rest for 10 minutes before shaping. The quick baking soda boil gives the pretzels their traditional flavor.

Ingredients

  • Pretzel Ingredients

    • 2¼ teaspoons instant yeast
    • 1 tablespoon brown sugar
    • 1½ cups warm water
    • 1 tablespoon unsalted butter
    • 1 teaspoon salted
    • 4 cups all-pourpose flour
    • Coarse salt, for topping

  • Baking Soda Bath Ingredients

    • 9 cups water
    • ½ cup baking soda

Directions

Make Dough

  1. In a large bowl whisk the yeast and sugar into warm water. Cover and sit for 1 minute
  2. Whisk in melted butter and salt.
  3. Add 3 cups flour and mix with a wooden spoon or dough hook in a standard mixer until combined.
  4. Add ¾ cup more flour until dough pulls away from the sides of the bowl. If it's still stick add up to ¼ cup more flour, 1 tablespoon at a time.
  5. Poke dough with your finger, if it bounces back it's ready to knead.

Knead Dough

  1. Keep dough in a mixer and beat for 3 to 5 full minutes, or knead by hand.
  2. If dough becomes too sticky during kneading sprinkle 1 teaspoon of flour at a time on the dough or the work surface. Don't use too much flour or the dough will become too dry.
  3. Do poke test again, if it slowly bounces back it is ready to rise.

Preheat oven to 400°F and line 2 baking sheets with parchment paper.

Shape Dough

  1. Shape dough into a ball.
  2. Cover lightly with a towel and allow to rest for 10 to 30 minutes. In the mean time you could make baking soda bath in step 5.
  3. Cut dough into 75g sections, which is about 1/3 cupo of dough each.
  4. Roll dough into a 20-22 inch rope.
  5. Form a circle with the dough by bringing the 2 ends together at the top of the circle.
  6. Twist the ends together.
  7. Bring the twisted ends back down towards yourself and press them down to form a pretzel shape.

Baking Soda Bath

  1. Add baking soda and 9 cups of water in a large pot
  2. Bring to a boil.
  1. Drop 1-2 pretzels into baking soda bath for 20-30 seconds. Any longer and your pretzels will have a metallic taste.
  2. Using a slotted spatula or spoon, remove pretzel from bath, allow excess water to drip off and place on prepared baking sheet.
  3. Sprinkle the top with coarse salt.
  4. Repeat with remaining pretzels.
